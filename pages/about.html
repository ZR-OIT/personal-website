About me

My interest in computers and electronics began in middle school when the family computer stopped working. We couldn't afford a new one at the time, so I had two options: find a way to fix it, or go without it. My mom wasn't enthusiastic about letting me try, but I insisted.

After hours of Googling and tinkering with the machine, I determined that the hard drive had failed and a new one with a fresh copy of Windows would get it up and running again. It worked, and although it seems like a small victory, this kickstarted my interest in computers and how they work under the hood.

Outside of computer hardware and programming, I find myself bouncing around from hobby to hobby and trying new things. At one moment I could be learning a game engine top to bottom and trying to make a game, and the next I could be outside trying to learn kickflips on my skateboard. Right now I really enjoy skateboarding, shooting, gaming, and 3D printing.

Education

I grew up in a small Oregon town called Tillamook, famous for the cheese and ice cream, where I graduated from Tillamook High School in 2015. I received a scholarship to attend the local community college, where I chose to pursue a basic associate's degree in science. After a couple weeks, I decided that community college wasn't right for me, and eventually applied to the Oregon Institute of Technology.

I began classes at Oregon Tech in the fall of 2017 in pursuit of a B.S. in Computer Engineering Technology. At the time, I didn't know if I was more interested in software or hardware, so CET seemed to provide a great mix of both. As a first generation college student, it was hard to adjust to a new environment hundreds of miles from home while working as many hours as my schedule allowed.

The end of my junior year was approaching when the COVID-19 pandemic swept the world, and suddenly every course went online and remote. Not only was I at the most challenging point in my degree, including junior and senior projects, I was even more isolated from the world and began to really struggle with my mental health. Unforunately, I failed an upper-level computer architecture class and was set back another year. However, when classes resumed in-person the following year, I was able to finish strong and receive my degree in June of 2022.

Work Experience

My work history began when I was 15 years old, saving to buy my first car. I worked at the Tillamook Cheese Factory until I graduated high school, which is when I decided to move on to a local lumbermill. During my time at the lumbermill, I decided I wanted to join the U.S. Navy. I excelled on the ASVAB and was offered a contract in Nuclear Engineering. When my flight to basic training was approaching, I ended up being disqualified due to a previous doctor visit for a long-lasting headache.

Devasted, I returned to Tillamook and went back to work at a family friend's gas station while I got back on my feet. Soon after, I ended up back at the lumbermill until it was time to move to Klamath Falls. Once in Klamath Falls, I quickly found a job at a local museum to help cover my expenses. I also worked side jobs on-campus for most of my time at Oregon Tech.

My work experience may seem all over the place, but it is mostly from starting young and still trying to decide what I want to do. In reality, I have always been a dependable and valuable employee, and my story brings it all together. Although I have no experience in fields relating to my degree, I have learned something of value from every job I have had such as customer service, team communication, attention to detail, and more.
